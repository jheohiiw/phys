Line Charge E-field (Integration)

Goal: Find E at P=(x,0) for a uniform line charge from y=-a to y=+a.

Step 1: Define dq
$$\lambda=\frac{Q}{2a}$$
$$dq=\lambda\,dy$$

Step 2: Geometry
$$r=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}$$
$$\cos\theta=\frac{x}{r}$$

Step 3: Coulomb's law for the small piece
$$dE=\frac{k\,dq}{r^2}=\frac{k\,dq}{x^2+y^2}$$

Step 4: Keep only x-component (symmetry cancels y-components)
$$dE_x=dE\cos\theta$$

Step 5: Substitute
$$dE_x=\frac{k\,dq}{x^2+y^2}\cdot\frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}
=\frac{kx\,dq}{(x^2+y^2)^{3/2}}$$

Step 6: Integrate with limits y=-a to a
$$E_x=\int_{-a}^{a}\frac{kx\,dq}{(x^2+y^2)^{3/2}}$$
$$E_x=\int_{-a}^{a}\frac{kx\left(\frac{Q}{2a}\right)}{(x^2+y^2)^{3/2}}\,dy$$

Step 7: Final result
$$E_x=\frac{kQ}{x\sqrt{x^2+a^2}}$$

Direction: +x if Q>0.
$$\cos \theta = \frac{\langle A,B \rangle}{\|A\|\|B\|}.$$
