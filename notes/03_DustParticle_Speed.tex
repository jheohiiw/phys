Electric Potential Energy → Speed

Problem (from figure):
A dust particle with mass m=2.50×10^{-9} kg and charge q0=3.0 nC starts from rest
and moves in a straight line from point a to point b.
The charges are +5.0 nC (left) and -5.0 nC (right).
Distances are 2.0 cm between each adjacent mark:
(+5.0 nC) — 2.0 cm — a — 2.0 cm — b — 2.0 cm — (-5.0 nC)
Find the speed at point b.

Given:
$$m=2.50\times10^{-9}\text{ kg}$$
$$q_0=3.0\text{ nC}=3.0\times10^{-9}\text{ C}$$
$$q_1=+5.0\text{ nC}=5.0\times10^{-9}\text{ C}$$
$$q_2=-5.0\text{ nC}=-5.0\times10^{-9}\text{ C}$$
$$k=8.99\times10^9\text{ N·m}^2/\text{C}^2$$

Convert cm to meters:
2.0 cm = 0.020 m

Positions along a line:
q1 at 0
a at 0.020 m
b at 0.040 m
q2 at 0.060 m

Distances:
At a: r_{1a}=0.020 m, r_{2a}=0.040 m
At b: r_{1b}=0.040 m, r_{2b}=0.020 m

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — Basic energy equation

Starts from rest:
$$K_i=0$$

Conservation:
$$K_i+U_i=K_f+U_f$$

So:
$$K_f=U_i-U_f$$

And:
$$K_f=\frac12 mv^2$$

Therefore:
$$\frac12 mv^2=U_i-U_f$$

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — Electric potential energy

For a test charge q0 in the potential of q1 and q2:

$$U=kq_0\left(\frac{q_1}{r_1}+\frac{q_2}{r_2}\right)$$

Compute U at a and b.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 3 — Compute U_a

$$U_a=kq_0\left(\frac{q_1}{r_{1a}}+\frac{q_2}{r_{2a}}\right)
=kq_0\left(\frac{5.0\times10^{-9}}{0.020}+\frac{-5.0\times10^{-9}}{0.040}\right)$$

Inside parentheses:
$$\frac{5.0\times10^{-9}}{0.020}=2.50\times10^{-7}$$
$$\frac{-5.0\times10^{-9}}{0.040}=-1.25\times10^{-7}$$

So:
$$\left(\cdots\right)=1.25\times10^{-7}$$

Thus:
$$U_a=(8.99\times10^9)(3.0\times10^{-9})(1.25\times10^{-7})
=3.37\times10^{-6}\text{ J}$$

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 4 — Compute U_b

$$U_b=kq_0\left(\frac{q_1}{r_{1b}}+\frac{q_2}{r_{2b}}\right)
=kq_0\left(\frac{5.0\times10^{-9}}{0.040}+\frac{-5.0\times10^{-9}}{0.020}\right)$$

Inside parentheses:
$$\frac{5.0\times10^{-9}}{0.040}=1.25\times10^{-7}$$
$$\frac{-5.0\times10^{-9}}{0.020}=-2.50\times10^{-7}$$

So:
$$\left(\cdots\right)=-1.25\times10^{-7}$$

Thus:
$$U_b=(8.99\times10^9)(3.0\times10^{-9})(-1.25\times10^{-7})
=-3.37\times10^{-6}\text{ J}$$

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 5 — Solve for v

$$\frac12 mv^2=U_a-U_b
=3.37\times10^{-6}-(-3.37\times10^{-6})
=6.74\times10^{-6}\text{ J}$$

$$v=\sqrt{\frac{2(6.74\times10^{-6})}{2.50\times10^{-9}}}
=\sqrt{5.39\times10^{3}}
=73.4\text{ m/s}$$

Final (3 sig figs):
$$\boxed{v=73.4\text{ m/s}}$$
