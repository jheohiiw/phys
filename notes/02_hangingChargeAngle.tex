Hanging Charge Angle (Equilibrium)

Problem:
A small spherical insulator of mass 6.00×10^{-2} kg and charge +0.400 µC
hangs on a thin wire. A charge of -0.220 µC is held 0.290 m away to the right.
Find the angle θ the wire makes with the vertical.

Given:
$$m=6.00\times10^{-2}\text{ kg}$$
$$q_1=+0.400\,\mu\text{C}=0.400\times10^{-6}\text{ C}$$
$$q_2=-0.220\,\mu\text{C}$$
$$r=0.290\text{ m}$$
$$k=8.99\times10^9\text{ N·m}^2/\text{C}^2$$
$$g=9.80\text{ m/s}^2$$

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — Basic equations (forces)

At equilibrium:
$$\sum F_x=0,\quad \sum F_y=0$$

Forces:
Weight: mg downward
Tension: T along string
Electric force: F_e horizontal (toward the negative charge)

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — Component equations

Horizontal:
$$T\sin\theta=F_e$$

Vertical:
$$T\cos\theta=mg$$

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 3 — Solve for θ

Divide the equations:

$$\tan\theta=\frac{F_e}{mg}$$

So:

$$\theta=\arctan\left(\frac{F_e}{mg}\right)$$

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 4 — Compute electric force with Coulomb’s law

Magnitude:
$$F_e=\frac{k|q_1q_2|}{r^2}$$

Compute:
$$|q_1q_2|=(0.400\times10^{-6})(0.220\times10^{-6})=0.0880\times10^{-12}=8.80\times10^{-14}$$

$$r^2=(0.290)^2=0.0841$$

$$F_e=\frac{(8.99\times10^9)(8.80\times10^{-14})}{0.0841}
=9.40\times10^{-3}\text{ N}$$

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 5 — Compute mg and θ

$$mg=(6.00\times10^{-2})(9.80)=0.588\text{ N}$$

$$\tan\theta=\frac{9.40\times10^{-3}}{0.588}=1.60\times10^{-2}$$

$$\theta=\arctan(1.60\times10^{-2})=0.916^\circ$$

Final (3 sig figs):
$$\boxed{\theta=0.916^\circ}$$
