Uniform Line Charge (Integration, not Gauss)

Problem:
Positive charge Q is distributed uniformly along the y-axis between y=-a and y=+a.
Find the electric field at point P on the x-axis at distance x from the origin.

Given:
Rod on y-axis from -a to +a
Point P = (x,0)

Goal:
Find E at P using integration.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — Basic equation (Coulomb’s law)

$$d\vec{E}=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{dq}{r^2}\hat{r}$$

We will integrate over the rod.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — Set up dq (uniform distribution)

Total length = 2a, total charge = Q

$$\lambda=\frac{Q}{2a}$$

A tiny piece of rod of thickness dy has charge:

$$dq=\lambda\,dy=\frac{Q}{2a}\,dy$$

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 3 — Geometry

Pick dq at location (0,y).
Point P is at (x,0).

Distance from dq to P:

$$r=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}$$

Direction:
By symmetry, the y-components cancel (equal and opposite).
So only the x-component survives.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 4 — Write dE_x

Magnitude:

$$dE=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{dq}{r^2}$$

x-component:

$$dE_x=dE\cos\theta$$

From the triangle:

$$\cos\theta=\frac{x}{r}$$

So:

$$dE_x=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{dq}{r^2}\cdot\frac{x}{r}
=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{x\,dq}{r^3}$$

Substitute r:

$$dE_x=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{x\,dq}{(x^2+y^2)^{3/2}}$$

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 5 — Integrate from y=-a to y=+a

$$E_x=\int_{-a}^{a}\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{x\,dq}{(x^2+y^2)^{3/2}}$$

Substitute dq:

$$E_x=\int_{-a}^{a}\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{x\left(\frac{Q}{2a}\right)}{(x^2+y^2)^{3/2}}\,dy$$

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 6 — Final answer (after evaluating the integral)

$$E_x=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{Q}{x\sqrt{x^2+a^2}}$$

Units:
$$\text{N/C}$$

Direction:
If Q>0, field points in +x direction.
